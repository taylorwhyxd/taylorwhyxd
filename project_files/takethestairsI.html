Take the Stairs —

How can we design to influence behavioral change? How can we convince people to switch to a healthier lifestyle? For the Take the Stairs campaign, I designed various styles of motivational signage in attempt to find a format, style, and messaging that correlates the biggest change in behavior. Data of stair climbing activity is collected using the OmniCounter Pro.

Designing for Behavioral Change —

I had the opportunity to attend a Behavioral Change Forum discussing the work of a Stanford Researcher, Bj Fogg. I was introduced to a behavioral change formula and learned about how I can create a successful campaign by leveraging people's innate tendency to create habits.

Behavioral Change Formula —

B (behavior) = M (motivation) + A (ability) + T (trigger)

Fogg, Bj. “A behavior model for persuasive design.”

Appeal to Values Messaging ↓

Through using messages that appeal to the audiences' values, I hope to form a connection and increase people's motivation level. The messaging used are sustainable-, motivational-, and fitness- related. I paired these messages up with appropriate buildings - for ex: Geography department — sustainability messaging.

Appeal to Perceived Ability ↓

Through creating simple signage, I hope to direct building occupants' attention right to the message rather than the graphics. In this particular set, my goal is to increase people's perceived ability to use the stairs through framing my words to make the act more feasible - for ex: "consider taking a few flights of stairs a day" and "squeeze in a quick exercise".
