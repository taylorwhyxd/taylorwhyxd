OmniCounter Pro —

The OmniCounter Pro is a people counting device, they are designed for facilities that need a reliable traffic counter with a flexible mounting options. This device is required for the UC-Wide initiative, Take the Stairs Campaign. Student coordinators from 10 UC schools will have to learn how to operate these devices through the given guide. In the process of figuring out how to use the counters, I found the company's how to guide to be wordy, lacking clear instructions, and containing confusing visuals..

Original Guide —

The original guide contains a heap of information about the different operational modes, the device's features and benefits, how to troubleshoot, and etc. Though it appears to be useful and informative, I found the formatting and the amount of words overwhelming. I also found myself calling the customer service line multiple times because some instructions were unclear. For example, the collecting counts instructions had one brief paragraph with no illustrations on where the "reed switch" is located.

The Redesigned Guide —

I focused on creating an illustrations-heavy guide with light texts because I wanted to lead the users with visuals rather than words. Since the device may look intimidating to some already, I thought it would be best to create a simple and to-the-point guide.

This how-to guide was created specifically to assist with the Take the Stairs Initiative. That said, it is most beneficial for students who are running this campaign, have limited time to learn how to operate the device, and need a quick go-to guide.
