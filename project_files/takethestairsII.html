Take the Stairs Residence Hall Campaign ‚Äî

The Take the Stairs Campaign is an effort of President Napolitano's UC Global Food Initiative. The campaign installs motivational signage at elevator banks to encourage students, staff, and faculty members to switch to a healthier and more energy efficient alternative of taking the stairs instead of the elevator. This spring, we are campaigning heavily in an on-campus dormitory. That said, our main target audience are first-year college students

Objective ‚Äî

To develop motivational signage and overall campaign around first-year college students at UC Santa Barbara


Messaging ‚Äî

I used bright colors on both the background and the illustrations to make the signage hard to miss. This is especially important because UCSB's dormitories often have many promotional signage about upcoming events, etc. right by the elevators. I also focused on using catchy phrases that are memorable and relevant; for example "The first wealth is health" (Ralph Waldo Emerson), and "Go green in your daily routine" both have rhyme patterns that make the phrases almost musical.

Stickers —

We focused on coming up with attention catching and witty stickers that college students would actually put on their laptop covers and/or hydroflasks. I aimed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner times when there is higher foot traffic since the dining commons are nearby.
