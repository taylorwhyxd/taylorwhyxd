Design Portfolio, Behind the Scene

My Website —

Welcome to my design portfolio! I love working on personal projects so redesigning and recoding my webpage was a ton of fun.

My main objective in redesigning a new portfolio site was to make my project pages easier to browse through. Since I am starting to add a lot more processes and pictures of my behind the scene work - I wanted to make each section clear and a breeze to follow.

Understanding my Audience —

The intended audience for my portfolio are hiring managers and talent acquisition professionals. Because of that, I focused on adding more behind the scene work and explanations to different projects. I wanted the intention behind each design decision to be clear and straightforward.

I also understand that, often times, hiring managers and talent acquisition professionals are busy and do not have a lot of time to browse through all my case studies/projects. That said- I divided my work into two chunks. The first section is a 2x2 grid focusing on recent projects that align my career goals. This section contains mostly product design work; they are also bigger visually compared to the second section. The second section is a 3x2 grid containing work that are mostly visual design heavy. I am equally proud of the projects in this section so I didn't want to leave them out of my portfolio completely. I wanted to show that I am a multidisciplinary designer who is also very familiar with Adobe CC in addition to Sketch, Principle, and InVision. As an early professional designer, I would like to demonstrate that I am able to wear different hats and open to learn more about various design disciplines.

Sketches —

I started out this project with putting my ideas on paper. To me, nothing is better than scribbling out all my thoughts and plans in a bullet journal using a sharp .5 mechanical pencil ✏️ This was a weekend project so I wanted to make all the coding very simple and reusable. I also believe reusing layouts will make my portfolio more visually consistent and cohesive - which in turn I hope will create a better experience for my visitors.

Layout —

From my sketches, I knew that I wanted all my main headers to be on the left side and the corresponding content on the right. Not knowing how it would look like with real content, I decided to map the whole layout out on Sketch. Having a visual with real data really helped me determine which layout was the easiest on the eyes.

I also laid out grids for the landing page to better viusalize how my projects would look like at first impression. I was choosing between two options: one with a gray consistent background and one with colored background unique to each project. I decided with the ladder because I thought it brought the projects to life and also added personality to the portfolio as a whole.

HTML & CSS —

I used HTML, CSS, and a CSS bootstrap (PureCSS) to create the page. The codes are very simple and straightforward. Responsive grids from PureCSS is the main foundation of this portfolio.

Final Thoughts —

This project is on-going! I plan on adding a few final touches down the road like transitions when scrolling and fun hover effects for the landing page. Stay tuned!
