Overview —

Introducing a social networking app that allows people to make plans, connect, and meet. Imagine making an adventurous decision and taking a solo trip to a country you’ve never been to before….or doing an exchange year somewhere you don’t have any connections and don’t speak the language. LetsMeet allows you to make plans with people in the same area, browse through plans that are made by others, and customize the plans and people you are interested in discovering.

Problems —

  1. Meeting new people can be difficult when traveling or moving to a new city where you don’t know anyone. Most of the times, you don’t entirely get to choose the people you meet.

  2. Let’s admit it, even when travelling with friends - sometimes not all of them are interested in doing the same thing as you do when traveling together.

  3. Existing apps for travellers mostly focus on finding accommodation (Couchsurfing), hooking up (Tinder), or were not created with the specific function of making and browsing plans made by strangers.

Solutions —

  1. Make Plans & Search Plans where it’s possible for users to customize and search plans based on location, the amount of people able to join, age of participant, gender of participant, and the amount of people the participant have met up with.

  2. Safe Meeting where users can adjust their discovery settings to see how many people the interested parties have met up with.

  3. Friendly Browsing and Viewing where plans and profiles are displayed largely and clearly so users can easily view profiles, read captions, and explore their options.

Final Thoughts & Potential Bugs —

  1. It could potentially be dangerous to openly make plans on a social media platform with strangers who you may not be interested in meeting up with. This could be solved by making the plans more ambiguous. Instead of stating the date, time, and exact location, users can provide a time frame and a suggestion of a place to meet up (i.e. lunch at a tapas place near the center of Valencia).

  2. The number of people a user have met up with may not be enough to provide credibility to their profile, therefore making the app unappealing to cautious individuals. Some ways that could make this problem a little less significant is to:

    • Connect users accounts to Facebook where common connections of 1st, 2nd, and 3rd degrees could be shown through mutual friends. This feature is already in practice on a dating mobile app, Tinder.
    • Allow users to report any unusual activities, harassment, and other unacceptable verbal exchanges (evidence through chats).
    • Provide an opportunity to rate – not by stars because not everyone is socially compatible with each other. Instead, provide a few questions to complete after the meet to ensure that the person is legitimate.
